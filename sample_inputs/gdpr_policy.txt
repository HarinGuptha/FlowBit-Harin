1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This policy establishes comprehensive guidelines for GDPR compliance within 
our organization to protect personal data and ensure data subject rights 
are respected in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation.

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, and third 
parties who process personal data of EU residents on behalf of our organization.

2. DATA PROTECTION PRINCIPLES
All personal data processing must adhere to the following principles:
- Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency
- Purpose limitation
- Data minimization
- Accuracy
- Storage limitation
- Integrity and confidentiality
- Accountability

3. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING
Personal data may only be processed under one of the following legal bases:
- Consent of the data subject
- Performance of a contract
- Compliance with legal obligations
- Protection of vital interests
- Performance of public tasks
- Legitimate interests

4. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
Data subjects have the following rights under GDPR:
- Right to be informed
- Right of access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure (right to be forgotten)
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object
- Rights related to automated decision making

5. DATA BREACH PROCEDURES
Any suspected personal data breach must be:
- Reported to the Data Protection Officer within 1 hour of discovery
- Assessed for risk to data subjects within 24 hours
- Reported to supervisory authorities within 72 hours if high risk
- Communicated to affected data subjects without undue delay if high risk

6. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS
Personal data may only be transferred outside the EU/EEA when:
- The destination country has an adequacy decision
- Appropriate safeguards are in place (BCRs, SCCs, etc.)
- Specific derogations apply

7. PRIVACY BY DESIGN
All new systems and processes must incorporate privacy by design principles:
- Proactive not reactive measures
- Privacy as the default setting
- Full functionality with privacy protection
- End-to-end security
- Visibility and transparency

8. TRAINING AND AWARENESS
All staff must complete GDPR training within 30 days of employment and 
annually thereafter. Specialized training is required for staff handling 
sensitive personal data.
